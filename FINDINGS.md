## 2. Ample Champion Quotient Analysis
- **Status**: REVISED — Original claim partially retracted after rigorous testing.
- **Target**: The "Ample Champion" polytope with $h_{1,1}=2, h_{2,1}=29, \chi=-54$.
- **Goal**: Find a freely acting discrete symmetry group of order 9 to quotient the manifold down to $\chi=-6$ (a 3-generation model).

### Corrections to Initial Analysis
- **Misidentification**: The polytope was initially described as $\mathbb{P}^2 \times \mathbb{P}^2$. This is **incorrect**. The standard $\mathbb{P}^2 \times \mathbb{P}^2$ polytope has $h_{2,1}=83, \chi=-162$. The KS polytope (h11=2, h21=29, chi=-54) is a different toric variety with 9 facets and 34 monomials, related to $\mathbb{P}^2 \times \mathbb{P}^2$ by a determinant-3 lattice transformation.

### What IS Verified (Genuine)
  - **Weights**: $w_1 = [0, 1, 1, 1, 0, 0]$, $w_2 = [1, 0, 0, 0, 1, 1]$
  - **Stanley-Reisner Ideal**: Generated by $\{x_0, x_4, x_5\}$ and $\{x_1, x_2, x_3\}$.
  - **$\mathbb{Z}_3 \times \mathbb{Z}_3$ Symmetry**: Genuinely exists. Both generators are lattice automorphisms with $\det(A) = 1$:
    - $g_1 = (0\ 4\ 5)$, $g_2 = (1\ 2\ 3)$ — commuting, order 3.
  - **Orbit Structure**: The 34 monomials correctly partition into 6 orbits (sizes: 1, 3, 3, 9, 9, 9).
  - **Combined Elements Act Freely**: The "diagonal" elements $g_1^a g_2^b$ (with $a \neq 0$ AND $b \neq 0$) have 0-dimensional fixed loci on the ambient toric variety, and a generic invariant polynomial is nonzero there (confirmed numerically 100/100 trials).

### What FAILS (Retracted)
  - **Pure $g_1$, $g_2$ Have Fixed Curves**: When only one cycle is active (pure $g_1$ or pure $g_2$), the "omega-fixed locus" on the toric variety is 2-dimensional. The CY hypersurface intersects this in a **curve** (a degree-3 polynomial on $\mathbb{P}^2$). This is a genuine fixed curve. Numerically confirmed: the optimizer found $|P|^2 \approx 10^{-88}$ at a point on the fixed locus.
  - **Full $\mathbb{Z}_3 \times \mathbb{Z}_3$ Quotient is SINGULAR**: The quotient $X / (\mathbb{Z}_3 \times \mathbb{Z}_3)$ has orbifold singularities along the images of the two fixed curves.
  - **$\chi = -6$ Not Directly Achieved**: The naive $\chi = -54/9 = -6$ is the orbifold Euler characteristic. The true $\chi$ of the resolved space differs.

### Salvaged Results
  - **Diagonal $\mathbb{Z}_3$ Quotient (Freely Acting)**:
    - The subgroup $\langle g_1 \circ g_2 \rangle \cong \mathbb{Z}_3$ acts **freely** on the CY (all elements have two disjoint 3-cycles, confirmed by numerical test).
    - $\chi(X/\mathbb{Z}_3) = -54/3 = -18$, giving $|\chi|/2 = 9$ generations.
    - Hodge numbers of the quotient: $h_{1,1} = 2, h_{2,1} = 11$ (both weight classes are invariant under $g_1 \circ g_2$).
  - **Orbifold Path**: The orbifold $X/(\mathbb{Z}_3 \times \mathbb{Z}_3)$ with its 2 singular curves (each a genus-1 curve) may still lead to interesting physics if resolved, but $\chi$ will change from $-6$.

### Resolved: h⁰ = 3 DOES NOT HOLD (h⁰ ≤ 2)
**Status**: CLOSED. Rigorously computed via Koszul exact sequence + lattice point counting + toric h¹ correction.

**Method**: For the CY hypersurface $X$ in the ambient toric 4-fold $V$:
1. The Koszul sequence $0 \to \mathcal{O}_V(D+K_V) \to \mathcal{O}_V(D) \to \mathcal{O}_X(D) \to 0$ gives:
   $h^0(X, D) = h^0(V, D) - h^0(V, D+K_V) + \dim\ker(H^1(V, D+K_V) \to H^1(V, D))$
2. $h^0(V, D)$ computed by counting lattice points in the polytope $P_D = \{m \in \mathbb{Z}^4 : \langle m, v_\rho \rangle \geq -d_\rho\}$ (19 fan rays, 4D ambient).
3. $h^1(V, D+K_V)$ computed via toric sheaf cohomology: counts disconnected components of $V(m) = \{\rho : \langle m, v_\rho \rangle < -l_\rho\}$ in the fan adjacency graph.

**Bug found in earlier script (40g)**: Off-by-one error — origin at polytope index 0 was removed but D_toric indexing wasn't shifted. Fixed in 40h.

**Results** (119 χ=+3 bundles, covering 1–4 divisors with coefficients up to ±3):
- **Maximum h⁰(X, D) = 2** (achieved by bundles like $D = e_0 + e_2$, $D = e_0 + e_6$, $D = e_0 + e_{10}$, and all 31 three-divisor bundles involving $e_0$).
- **h¹(V, D+K_V) = 0** for ALL bundles → Koszul formula is **exact**, not just a lower bound.
- **No Kodaira vanishing pathway**: Zero nef+big bundles among all 246 χ=±3 bundles (closest to nef: min Mori pairing = −2). So Kawamata-Viehweg vanishing cannot prove h⁰ = χ.
- **cohomCalg blocked**: 97 Stanley-Reisner generators exceed cohomCalg's hard limit of 64.

**Consistency check**: For $D$ with $h^0 = 2$ and $\chi = 3$: Serre duality gives $h^3 = h^0(-D) = 0$ (since $-D$ is "negative"), so $2 - h^1 + h^2 = 3$, i.e., $h^2 - h^1 = 1$. This is consistent and means the NET generation count from this bundle is $|h^1 - h^2| = 1$, not 3.

**Important clarification**: The 3-generation claim for Polytope 40 comes from the CY Euler characteristic $|\chi(X)|/2 = |h^{1,1} - h^{2,1}| = |15 - 18| = 3$ (standard embedding), NOT from any line bundle having $h^0 = 3$. The pipeline conflated these two distinct statements.
